The Four Color Theorem meets Shapes of Polyhedra

Richard Evan Schwartz

arXiv:2604.12059·math.MG·Published 2026-04-13

We consider solutions to the $4$-color problem for the vertices of sphere triangulations with degree sequence $6,...,6,4,4,4,4,4,4$. We sort these solutions into combinatorial types and show that each generic type $τ$ is parametrized by the set of integer lattice points inside a $4$-dimensional rational polyhedral convex cone ${\cal C\/}_τ$. There is an integral quadratic form $Q_τ$ on ${\cal C\/}_τ$ whose diagonal part, evaluated on a lattice point, is $3$ times the number of triangles in the corresponding triangulation. We relate this structure to the octahedral stratum of Thurston's moduli space of flat cone structures on the sphere.
