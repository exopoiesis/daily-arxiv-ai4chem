Protein folding as a jamming transition

Alex T. Grigas, Zhuoyi Liu, Jack A. Logan, Mark D. Shattuck, Corey S. O'Hern

arXiv:2405.09646·cond-mat.soft·Published 2024-05-15

Proteins fold to a specific functional conformation with a densely packed hydrophobic core that controls their stability. We develop a geometric, yet all-atom model for proteins that explains the universal core packing fraction of $φ_c=0.55$ found in experimental measurements. We show that as the hydrophobic interactions increase relative to the temperature, a novel jamming transition occurs when the core packing fraction exceeds $φ_c$. The model also recapitulates the global structure of proteins since it can accurately refold to native-like structures from partially unfolded states.
