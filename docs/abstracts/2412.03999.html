Unveiling the origin of diffusion suppression of hydrogen isotopes at the α-Al2O3(0001)/α-Cr2O3(0001) interfaces

Yuji Kunisada, Ryotaro Sano, Norihito Sakaguchi

arXiv:2412.03999·cond-mat.mtrl-sci·Published 2024-12-05

It has been reported that the α-Al2O3, a promising tritium permeation barrier material for a fusion reactor, can be grown at low temperatures on the α-Cr2O3 template, and that α-Al2O3/α-Cr2O3 composite films have more efficiently suppress the hydrogen isotope permeation than the single α-Al2O3 film. In this study, we investigated the diffusion properties of hydrogen isotopes at the α-Al2O3(0001)/α-Cr2O3(0001) interfaces using first-principles calculations based on density functional theory. In the α-Al2O3 region near the interfaces, O-H covalent bonds, which are not observed in the bulk α-Al2O3, are formed, and hydrogen isotopes become stable. Such chemical bonds induced by the interfaces are the origin of hydrogen isotope trapping and result in a larger diffusion barrier than in the α-Al2O3 and the α-Cr2O3. It was also found that the suppression of hydrogen isotope diffusion does not occur at the interface site but at sites adjacent to the interfaces. In addition, the interface enhances the oxygen vacancies, which may also suppress hydrogen isotope permeation.
